The Lewis BB2-Ti is a titanium dual-piston flat mount brake caliper that weighs 76g per caliper. A Dura-Ace caliper is around 100g. That's roughly 50g per wheel from a part most people never think to swap. I've been running them on my Aethos weight weenie build and they work, but the braking is noticeably worse than Shimano.

### Overview

Weights are per caliper, without pads.

|   | Lewis BB2-Ti | Trickstuff C22 | Shimano Dura-Ace (BR-R9270) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Weight (per caliper) | ~76g | ~81g | ~100g |
| Price (per caliper) | ~$119 | ~€300 | ~$190 |
| Material | Titanium | Aluminum | Aluminum |
| Pistons | 2 | 2 | 2 |
| Mount | Flat mount | Flat mount | Flat mount |

The Trickstuff C22 is the obvious comparison. It's only 5g heavier and by all accounts brakes much better, but it's almost triple the price. At $119 per caliper, the Lewis undercuts both the Trickstuff and Dura-Ace on price while being the lightest of the three.

### The braking problem

The stock pads squealed like crazy on longer descents. I swapped to SRAM pads which quieted them down, but the braking itself is still the issue. The lever feel is very on/off — there's not much between "not braking" and "braking hard." Shimano brakes are way more progressive. You can feather them. These, not so much.

They also overheat on long descents. If you're dragging brakes for extended periods in the mountains, these will fade. On flat criteriums or shorter climbs it's not a problem, but on anything with real elevation it's worth thinking about.

### Setup notes

A few things I ran into during installation:

- **Pad compatibility:** The BB2-Ti uses standard Shimano-style road pads (L03A/L05A or equivalent). The SRAM pads I ended up running are the same form factor. Metallic or semi-metallic pads will help with heat but are louder.
- **Bleed process:** Lewis calipers use mineral oil, same as Shimano. If you're already on Shimano levers, the bleed process is identical. I didn't need to re-bleed when swapping from Dura-Ace calipers — just connected the hose and bled normally.
- **Rotor compatibility:** Works with both 140mm and 160mm rotors. I'm running them with [Galfer Wave rotors](/galfer-wave-center-lock-rotors/) in 160/140 front/rear.

### Verdict

Race-day only, and even then only on courses without serious descending. You're saving almost 50g per caliper, but you're giving up a lot of braking to get there. The on/off feel and the heat fade make these a bad idea for everyday riding. Hill climb bike or flat crit bike where you barely touch the brakes? Sure. Anything else, stick with Dura-Ace.

If budget isn't a concern and you want lightweight calipers that actually brake well, the Trickstuff C22 is probably the better call at 81g — but you're paying €300 per caliper for that privilege.
